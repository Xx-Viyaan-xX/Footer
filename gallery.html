1. Prescription Glasses Single Vision Glasses: These have one prescription for the entire lens, suitable for nearsightedness, farsightedness, or astigmatism. Bifocals: These lenses have two distinct sections: the top part for distance vision and the bottom part for close-up tasks. Progressive Lenses: These offer a gradual transition between different prescriptions, accommodating distance, intermediate, and near vision without visible lines.

2. Reading Glasses Magnification Glasses: Designed specifically for close-up tasks like reading. They come in various strengths depending on your magnification needs.

3. Safety Glasses Industrial Safety Glasses: Equipped with impact-resistant lenses and sturdy frames to protect eyes in industrial environments. Sports Goggles: Designed to protect eyes during sports activities, often with shatterproof lenses and secure straps.

4. Sunglasses Polarized Sunglasses: Reduce glare from reflective surfaces like water or roads, improving visibility and reducing eye strain. UV Protection Sunglasses: Block harmful ultraviolet rays to protect your eyes from sun damage.

5. Blue Light Glasses Anti-Blue Light Glasses: Filter out blue light emitted by screens and digital devices, potentially reducing eye strain and improving sleep quality.

6. Computer Glasses Computer Glasses: Designed for those who spend long hours in front of screens. They often have anti-reflective coatings to reduce glare and may be optimized for intermediate vision.

7. Fashion Glasses Eyewear as Accessories: Some glasses are primarily designed to enhance style or make a fashion statement rather than correct vision. They may include non-prescription lenses or be purely decorative.

8. Specialty Glasses Photochromic Lenses: Darken in response to sunlight and clear up indoors, providing comfort in varying light conditions. Safety Goggles: Used for specific hazards like chemical splashes or high-impact situations. They provide a snug fit and additional protection around the eyes.



9. Contact Lenses Daily Wear Contacts: Replaceable daily lenses for ongoing vision correction. Extended Wear Contacts: Can be worn continuously for longer periods, even overnight. Rigid Gas Permeable (RGP) Contacts: Durable lenses that provide sharp vision and can be an option for some people with specific eye conditions.
